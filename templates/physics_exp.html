The Doppler Effect

The Doppler Effect is the apparent change in the charachteristicks of a wave due to relative motion between the source of the wave and the observer. It can be observed in both, mechanical and electromagnetic waves.

Our universe, probably not the only universe, is constantly expanding, hence; the space in the universe is also expanding. As a result of this expansion, large-scale entitites such as gallaxies, are moving away from each other.

Red-shifting

When an object is moving away from the observer, the apparent wavelength increases and the frequency decreases. This phenomenon is known as red-shoftomg, as the EM wave moves towards the red side on the color guage. Light from most distant objects in our universe tends to be red-shifted by a large factor - calculations show that some infrared waves that we observe were ultraviolet at the time of formation but have been red-shifted since.

Blue-shifting

When an object is moving towards the observer, the apparent wavelength increases and the frequency decreases. This phenomenon is known as blue-shoftomg, as the EM wave moves towards the blue side on the color guage. Light from most distant objects in our universe tends to be red-shifted by a large factor - calculations show that some infrared waves that we observe were ultraviolet at the time of formation but have been red-shifted since.
